🔬 The statistical smoking gun

One feature explains almost half the ROAS variance.

We trained a Random Forest on 27 joined creatives and 130 creative features. The top-ranked feature alone carries 43.5% of total feature importance. In an ANOVA, claim_type explains 64% of ROAS variance. Medical claim count correlates with ROAS at ρ = 0.70 (p < 0.001).

43.5%
of total feature importance sits on a single variable: whether the creative makes a medical / authority-style claim. Everything else combined explains the other 56.5%.

Six root-cause observationsWhy 2026 hurts, ranked by evidence strength

OBSERVATION #1 · Attention gap

ECR collapsed 45% year-over-year

2.22% → 1.22%

Engagement-continuation — the fraction of impressions that didn't swipe — dropped from 2.22% to 1.22%. Every downstream number flows from this signal. The feed isn't stopping on 2026 ads as often as it did on 2025 ads.

OBSERVATION #2 · Format collapse

INT-UGC lost 51% of its ROAS

0.30 → 0.15

Interactive talking-head UGC was the 2025 workhorse at $443,414 spend. In 2026, $73,091 produced ROAS 0.15. ECR on INT fell from 2.37% to 0.97%. The format has been copied to exhaustion.

OBSERVATION #3 · Hero drought

No 2026 creative approaches 2025's breakout

$238,935 → $27,819

The 2025 top creative spent 26% of the year's budget on a single ad at 0.33 ROAS. 2025's top 3 took 42% of budget. 2026's biggest is $27,819. Portfolio has diversified, but lost concentration behind a proven hero.

OBSERVATION #4 · Pillar theme abandoned

Liver + blood-sugar collapsed 96%

$216,390 → $8,998

2025's two anchor pain-points — liver ($111,329, ROAS 0.27) and blood sugar ($105,061, ROAS 0.27) — got replaced by cortisol ($62,989 at ROAS 0.13). Cortisol pays less than half.

OBSERVATION #5 · One bright spot

Sale/price angle is 2026's saviour

0.39 ROAS

Sale/discount was marginal in 2025 ($8,000, 0.14 ROAS). In 2026 it became 40% of spend ($130,397) at ROAS 0.39 — the best theme of either year. Both top-2 2026 ads are sale-angle.

OBSERVATION #6 · Format to scale

M-UGC emerged as a repeatable winner

0.20 → 0.38

M-UGC (mobile / motion UGC) was a rounding error in 2025. In 2026 it grew 4× in spend to $75,741 at 0.38 ROAS — the best UGC format of either year. The #1 2026 creative is M-UGC. Scale this, not INT.

Method · how these numbers are built

Sources. Viscap_raw_data_2025_vs_2026.xlsx (P30–P34 + P45–P48 ad rows from Northbeam) + per_video.parquet (27 creatives scored by video-analyzer v2: Gemini-3-flash, temperature 0.15, seed 42).

Weighted metrics. All ROAS / CPO / ECR / CPM figures are spend-weighted. Each ad row contributes in proportion to its dollars spent, so a $100 test doesn't dilute a $50k scale test.

Statistical tests. Random Forest trained on 26 joined creatives, target = weighted_roas_new, 17+ categorical and numeric features. Permutation importance reported. ANOVA on claim_type. Spearman rank correlations for robustness against outliers. Hypothesis tests H1–H15 flagged by Hellinger distance (categorical distributions), mean comparisons, and Wilcoxon tests.

Caveats. n=26 is exploratory. Two creatives ran both years with ~$0 2026 spend — we cannot separate audience fatigue from creative decay in those cases. 2026 budget is ~$0.35M vs 2025's $0.93M, so 2026 is a partial sample of a test phase, not a full comparison window.

Generated 2026-04-17 18:19 UTC. Data: 658 Northbeam rows (2025) + 314 rows (2026) + 27 Gemini-scored creatives. Feature importance, ANOVA, and Spearman values drawn from findings.md (analys/outputs) — a separate Python pipeline computed them against the full joined dataset.
