Why 2026 is softer — six observationsRoot-cause hypothesis after decomposing 972 ad rows

OBSERVATION #1 · Attention gap

ECR collapsed 45% year-over-year

2.22% → 1.22%

Engagement-continuation — the share of impressions that didn't swipe away — dropped from 2.22% in 2025 to 1.22% in 2026. This is not a ROAS problem yet — it's the input signal for the whole funnel. Less continuation → fewer visits → fewer orders per $ spent. Every downstream number flows from this.

OBSERVATION #2 · Format collapse

INT-UGC lost 51% of its ROAS

0.30 → 0.15

Interactive UGC — talking-head creators with high visual variety — was the 2025 workhorse at $443,414 spend and ROAS 0.30. In 2026 the same format drew only $73,091 and ROAS 0.15. ECR on INT fell from 2.37% to 0.97%. Audiences have seen this format a thousand times.

OBSERVATION #3 · Hero-creative drought

No 2026 creative approaches 2025's breakout

$239k → $28k

The top 2025 ad (IT_MC_32_B Silver Wells — "Did you guys see that video of one girl crying") spent $238,935 at 0.33 ROAS — 26% of all 2025 spend on a single creative. 2025's top 3 ads took 42% of budget. 2026's biggest creative is $27,819. The portfolio is spread thin across many mid-performers instead of concentrated behind a hero.

OBSERVATION #4 · Pillar theme abandoned

Liver + blood-sugar went from $216,390 to $8,998

$207,392 of proven angles lost

2025's two anchor pain-points — liver ($111,329, 0.27 ROAS) and blood sugar ($105,061, 0.27 ROAS) — were replaced by cortisol ($62,989, 0.13 ROAS) as the dominant health angle. Cortisol pays less than half. The pivot away from what worked wasn't matched by a same-efficacy replacement.

OBSERVATION #5 · One bright spot

Sale/price angle is 2026's saviour

0.39 ROAS

The sale/discount angle was marginal in 2025 ($8,000 spend, 0.14 ROAS) but became 40% of 2026 spend ($130,397) at ROAS 0.39 — the highest of any theme either year. The top two 2026 ads are both sale-angle creatives. This is a rare upside: price-sensitivity messaging is working when health-problem messaging isn't.

OBSERVATION #6 · Format to double down on

M-UGC emerged as a scalable winner

0.20 → 0.38 ROAS

Mobile/motion UGC was a rounding error in 2025 (30 rows, $20,524 at ROAS 0.20). In 2026 it grew 4× in spend to $75,741 and nearly doubled ROAS to 0.38 — the best UGC format of either year. The top 2026 ad (Jordan "How much does this cost?") is M-UGC. This is the format to scale, not INT-UGC.

Three things the losers did that the winners didn't

Loser pattern · Hook

Question hooks invite thinking — the feed punishes thinking

2 of 5

Loser cohort opened with a question 40% of the time. Winners: zero. Swap questions for bold claims.

Loser pattern · Narrative

Third-person narration feels like a podcast intro

2 of 5

Losers used 3rd-person narration; every winner used first-person. One loser didn't reveal the product until 87% through the video. First-person or don't ship.

Winner pattern · Pacing

Cut every 1.7 seconds — don't let them breathe

38 cuts/min

Winners averaged 38/min vs 31 for losers. Anatomical animations, b-roll inserts, text overlays — reset attention before boredom sets in.

Winner pattern · CTA

Risk-reversal converts. Urgency bounces.

money-back

Every winning creative closed with a 30-day money-back guarantee framed "Risk-Free". Losers leaned on scarcity + countdown language. Safety converts; pressure pushes back.

Method · how the numbers are built

Raw data: Viscap_raw_data_2025_vs_2026.xlsx, two sheets (P30–P34 for 2025, P45–P48 for 2026). All ROAS/CPO/ECR/CPM figures are spend-weighted — each row contributes in proportion to its dollars. Creative decode: 36 videos scored by video-analyzer v2 (Gemini, temperature 0.15, seed 42), joined to ad rows via core ID extraction (format phase_variant_ugc-type_number). Winner/loser cohorts: top 5 and bottom 5 by spend-weighted ROAS across 22 ads with min $50 spend.

Observations at these sample sizes (n≈22) are exploratory — good for hypothesis generation, not causal inference. Re-test every claim in a 2026 ad split.

Generated 2026-04-17 18:01 UTC. Q1 2025 window: P30–P34 (658 rows). Q1 2026 window: P45–P48 (314 rows). 24 of 36 analysed creatives matched to ad-spend data.
